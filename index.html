Gjergj Kastriot Skenderbeu

The man who saved Albania

Skanderbeg was a 15th-century Albanian nobleman and military commander who successfully resisted Ottoman conquests in the Balkans for over two decades.

Here is a time line of Skanderbeg's life:

  • May 6, 1405: Skanderbeg (born Gjergj Kastrioti) is born in the village of Sinë, Albania.
  • 1423: Skanderbeg is sent to the Ottoman court as a hostage, where he receives military training and serves in the Ottoman army.
  • 1443: Skanderbeg defects from the Ottoman army and returns to Albania, where he begins organizing resistance against Ottoman rule.
  • November 28, 1443:Skanderbeg raises the standard of rebellion and declares a holy war against the Ottomans.
  • 1444: Skanderbeg wins his first major victory against the Ottomans at the Battle of Torvioll.
  • 1450: Skanderbeg establishes the League of Lezhë, a confederation of Albanian princes and lords who unite under his leadership to resist the Ottomans.
  • 1461: Skanderbeg dies of malaria while fighting the Ottomans.
  • 1479:The Ottomans conquer Albania and bring an end to the resistance movement led by Skanderbeg.

"I did not fight against the Turks for my own sake alone, but for Christianity and the safety of all Europe."

--Skanderbeg
